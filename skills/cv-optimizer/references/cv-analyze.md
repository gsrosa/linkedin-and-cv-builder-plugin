# Analyze CV (holistic pass)

Use before strengths/weaknesses and before rewriting. Goal: understand the **whole document** — not individual bullets yet.

## Objective

Produce a structured snapshot of the CV: what is present, what is missing, and whether layout and content align with the **stated target role** (or infer one from the user).

## What to review

### 1. Document structure

- Expected order (typical): name/contact → title → summary → experience → skills → education → languages (if relevant)
- Sections present vs. missing (e.g. no summary, no links, skills as a flat wall of keywords)
- Length: one vs. two pages for senior roles; density vs. whitespace

### 2. Header & title line

- Title specific enough? (e.g. “Senior X Engineer” vs. “Software Engineer” only)
- Location / remote signal if relevant
- Contact: email, LinkedIn, GitHub/portfolio if applicable

### 3. Professional summary

- Opens with identity and specialty vs. “seeking opportunities”
- Years + domain + proof in a few lines
- Banned fluff: “passionate”, “team player”, “fast learner” as substitutes for substance

### 4. Experience section

- Reverse chronological order
- Each role: company context (one line if unknown), dates, title
- Bullets: mix of impact vs. task-only lines (flag ratio)
- Obvious gaps: no metrics anywhere, only responsibilities, overlapping dates unexplained

### 5. Skills

- Categorized vs. undifferentiated list
- Alignment with headline and experience (no random keyword stuffing)

### 6. Education & extras

- Proportion vs. experience (senior CVs: keep education short)
- Certifications only if relevant

### 7. Target role alignment

- Keywords and themes that match job descriptions the user cares about
- Gaps: missing proof for claimed seniority (leadership, scale, ownership)

## Output format

- **Structure:** bullet list of sections and what each contains (1–2 lines each)
- **Gaps:** missing sections, weak sections, structural issues
- **Alignment:** fit to target role (or assumptions stated clearly)
- **Next step:** point to [cv-review-strengths-weaknesses.md](cv-review-strengths-weaknesses.md), then bullet-level files as needed
