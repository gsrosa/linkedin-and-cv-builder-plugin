# DevOps / platform / SRE — positioning examples

Use when the user’s primary track is **DevOps**, **platform**, **SRE**, **cloud infrastructure**, or **reliability engineering**. Override every pattern with their cloud(s), scale, and measurable outcomes.

## What strong DevOps / platform positioning looks like

- Ties work to **outcomes**: deploy frequency, MTTR, cost, security posture, developer experience — not only tool names.
- Shows **ownership** of platforms, pipelines, or production readiness.

## Weak → strong (table)

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| DevOps engineer with AWS and Kubernetes | Platform engineer: **Kubernetes-based delivery and GitOps** — standardized clusters and reduced mean time to recovery for production incidents by [X] |
| CI/CD expert | SRE focused on **SLOs, alerting hygiene, and incident response** — introduced error budgets and blameless reviews tied to measurable reliability targets |

## Content pillar ideas

- CI/CD that teams actually adopt (not only YAML samples)
- Infrastructure as code, secrets, and least privilege
- On-call culture, incident reviews, and reducing alert noise
- Cost and performance of cloud estates; FinOps angles when credible

## Headline patterns

- `[Seniority] Platform / DevOps / SRE \| [AWS/GCP/Azure] · K8s \| [focus: e.g. GitOps · observability · reliability]`
- `SRE \| [scale or industry] \| SLOs · incident response · [Terraform/Pulumi…]`
